Mass Communication

Mass Communication is concerned with collection and dissemination of news through the print media as well as the electronic media. This involves various areas of works like reporting, writing, editing, photographing, broadcasting or cable casting news items. Job prospects after Mass Communication may include:

-> Journalism Journalism is through which an individual makes information accessible to the public. These people work for independent enterprises or national press

-> Advertising Advertising is a visual or audio form of marketing communication which aims at promoting or selling a product.

-> Disc Jockey Disc Jockeys are artists that mix recorded tapes as it is playing. They also record their mixes for later sales and distribution.

-> Film-Making Film making is nothing but the process of making a film involving production, recording and editing which are further more divided in various parts.

-> Public Relations Public Relations means passing information from an individual or an organisation to the public.

-> Publishing and Printing Publishing is a dissemination of information, literature or music which is published or printed in magazines or newspapers to make it accessible to general public.

-> Radio Jockeying Radio Jockey is a person who has on air position in radio broadcasting.

-> Video Jockeying Video Jockey is a person who introduces music ad new videos to commercial television. Read on to learn about the available training courses, job opportunities and earning potential of a Video jockey.

-> Video Editing Video Editing is the process of editing and manipulating video images and clips.

Qualification requirements


  • Mass Communication can be pursued after any stream in Class 12th.
