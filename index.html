Breaking a few Myths:-

We often tend to hold certain misconceptions about career options, which end up colouring the choices we make. Hence, I am listing a few of the commonly held myths, and also making an attempt to clarify them with appropriate facts in order to facilitate proper decision making.



    Myth no. 1:- “Opting for Science in Class XI will keep all career options open for me.”

  • Fact: We often opt for Science thinking that all career options shall remain open henceforth; however, once science is opted for, just the opposite happens. We end up closing down all other options in our mind and target just one, namely, Engineering.


    • Myth no. 2- “Humanities holds no career options and will ultimately lead to academic doom.”

  • Fact: Humanities offers some career options such as Liberal Arts; Law; Psychology and Social Work, along with many more, which are not only monetarily lucrative and immensely in demand, but also very self-satisfying for the person pursuing them, given that the person has a genuine inclination towards them.


    • Myth no. 3- “Creative careers like Design and Mass Communication have no scope.”

  • Fact: In today’s scenario of globalisation and academic growth, there is hardly any field which does not have “scope”. People who have a genuine interest and aptitude for creative fields may flourish in all possibility, given that they put in their best foot forward which is a prerequisite for any field.


    • Myth no. 4- “Commerce without Maths holds no future.”

  • Fact: We are currently in such a liberal academic scenario where the absence of one subject cannot predict the career growth for a student. A student pursuing Commerce, without maths, has a plethora of career options for him/her, including CA.
